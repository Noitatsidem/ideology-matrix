Equality: Do you prefer free markets with minimal interference, or active government efforts to reduce economic inequality?

Liberty: How much power should the state have over individuals? More authority for order and safety, or more personal freedom?

Progress: Do you value traditional customs and institutions, or embrace social change and modern values?

Identity: Do you prioritize your nation's interests and borders, or see yourself as a global citizen?

Populism: Do you trust experts and institutions to make decisions, or believe ordinary people should have more direct power?

Intervention: Should your country focus on domestic issues, or actively engage in foreign affairs and military action?

Ecology: Should human needs come first, or does nature have value beyond what it provides to people?

Power: Should authority be centralized for efficiency, or spread out among local communities?

Change: Should society change slowly and carefully, or are rapid or dramatic changes sometimes needed?

Human Nature: Are people fundamentally fixed in their nature, or can education and good institutions improve us?

Means: Is achieving results worth compromising your values, or should principles always come first?

Property: Should property be privately owned, or should communities share ownership of key resources?

Secularism: Should religion influence public life and policy, or should government be strictly secular?

Technology: Are you cautious about new technologies, or optimistic about their potential to solve problems?

Welfare: Should government help only those who qualify, or provide universal benefits to everyone?

Integration: Should newcomers adopt the majority culture, or should society celebrate diverse cultural identities?
