Ideology Matrix

A comprehensive political values test grounded in political science



What is Ideology Matrix?

Ideology Matrix is a political values test that measures your positions across sixteen independent axes derived from political science research. Unlike simpler political compasses, it distinguishes between concepts that are often conflated—like economic regulation vs. economic equality, or cultural progressivism vs. civil libertarianism.

You will be presented with statements and asked to rate your agreement from Strongly Agree to Strongly Disagree. Your answers are compared against the maximum possible scores to give you a percentage on each axis.

There are questions in the test.

The Sixteen Axes

Each axis is based on established political science frameworks including the Chapel Hill Expert Survey, Inglehart-Welzel World Values Survey, Akkerman Populism Scale, Sowell's "Conflict of Visions," Ostrom's commons research, and welfare state typologies.

FREE MARKET

Belief in market mechanisms, private enterprise, low regulation, and economic freedom. Skeptical of redistribution and government economic intervention.

EQUALITY

ECONOMIC EQUALITY

Belief in wealth redistribution, social programs, progressive taxation, and reducing economic inequality. Support for public services and worker protections.

STATE AUTHORITY

Acceptance of state power over individuals for security, order, or social goals. May support surveillance, censorship, or restrictions on personal freedoms.

LIBERTY

CIVIL LIBERTY

Strong belief in individual rights, privacy, freedom of speech and expression. Skeptical of government surveillance and restrictions on personal choices.

TRADITIONAL

Value for established customs, religious institutions, traditional family structures, and cultural continuity. Skeptical of rapid social change.

PROGRESS

PROGRESSIVE

Embrace of social change, secular values, gender equality, LGBTQ+ rights, and evolving social norms. Prioritize reason and science over tradition.

NATIONALIST

Priority for national identity, sovereignty, and borders. Skeptical of immigration, international organizations, and globalization. "Citizens first" mentality.

IDENTITY

COSMOPOLITAN

Embrace of global citizenship, open borders, international cooperation, and multiculturalism. View humanity as one community beyond national boundaries.

PLURALIST

Trust in institutions, experts, and representative democracy. Value compromise, checks and balances, and incremental change through established channels.

POPULISM

POPULIST

Belief that "ordinary people" are opposed by a "corrupt elite." Distrust of mainstream institutions, media, and experts. Favor direct democracy and outsider politics.

NON-INTERVENTIONIST

Oppose military intervention abroad. Favor diplomacy, reduced defense spending, and focusing on domestic issues. Skeptical of military alliances and foreign entanglements.

INTERVENTION

INTERVENTIONIST

Support for active foreign policy, military strength, and willingness to intervene abroad for security or humanitarian goals. Value alliances and global influence.

HUMAN-CENTERED

Human interests take priority over environmental concerns. Skeptical of climate action that impedes economic growth. Trust technology to solve environmental problems.

ECOLOGY

NATURE-CENTERED

Nature has intrinsic value. Strong support for environmental protection, climate action, and sustainability—even at economic cost. May support animal rights.

CONCENTRATED

Preference for centralized, efficient governance. Strong national authority, unified decision-making, and clear hierarchies. Skeptical of federalism and local autonomy.

POWER

DISTRIBUTED

Preference for decentralized power, federalism, local autonomy, and direct democracy. Support for checks and balances and participatory decision-making.

GRADUALIST

Preference for slow, incremental reform within existing systems. Trust in institutions' ability to evolve. Skeptical of rapid or revolutionary change.

CHANGE

REVOLUTIONARY

Belief that fundamental change requires breaking with existing systems. Willingness to pursue rapid, sweeping transformation of society.

FIXED HUMAN NATURE

View of human nature as fixed and flawed. Society must work within limits of human selfishness. Skeptical of utopian social engineering.

HUMAN NATURE

IMPROVABLE HUMAN NATURE

View of human nature as malleable and perfectible. With proper institutions, education, and environment, most social problems can be solved.

RESULTS-FOCUSED

Focus on practical results over ideological purity. Willingness to compromise and adapt. "What works" matters more than theoretical consistency.

MEANS

VALUES-FOCUSED

Commitment to moral principles regardless of consequences. Means matter as much as ends. Certain actions are inherently right or wrong.

PRIVATE

Strong belief in private property rights. Individual ownership creates responsibility, efficiency, and freedom. Skeptical of collective ownership models.

PROPERTY

COMMONS

Belief that key resources should be commonly owned or managed. Support for cooperatives, public ownership, and shared stewardship of resources.

RELIGIOUS

Religion should inform public life and policy. Faith communities provide moral guidance essential for society. Skeptical of pure secularism.

SECULARISM

SECULAR

Strict separation of religion and state. Public policy should be based on reason and evidence, not religious doctrine. Morality doesn't require faith.

TECH SKEPTIC

Cautious about technological solutions. Technology often creates new problems. Value traditional ways of living. Skeptical of tech-driven progress.

TECHNOLOGY

TECH ENTHUSIAST

Optimistic about technology's potential to solve problems. Support for innovation, automation, and technological advancement as keys to progress.

TARGETED

Social programs should be means-tested and directed at those in need. Personal responsibility first; assistance as a safety net, not an entitlement.

WELFARE

UNIVERSAL

Social programs should be universal rights available to all. Universal programs build solidarity and avoid stigma. Support for comprehensive welfare state.

SHARED CULTURE

Immigrants and minorities should adopt the majority culture. National cohesion requires shared identity. Skeptical of multiculturalism.

INTEGRATION

CULTURAL DIVERSITY

Cultural diversity should be celebrated and preserved. Multiple identities can coexist. Support for minority rights and cultural accommodation.


Methodology

This test draws on several established frameworks in political science:

  • Chapel Hill Expert Survey (CHES) - Standard for measuring party positions in Europe
  • Inglehart-Welzel World Values Survey - Cross-cultural value dimensions
  • Akkerman Populism Scale - Validated populism measurement
  • New Ecological Paradigm Scale - Environmental attitudes research
  • Dahl's Polyarchy - Democratic theory on power distribution
  • Sowell's "Conflict of Visions" - Constrained vs unconstrained vision of human nature
  • Ostrom's Commons Research - Property and resource management theory
  • Esping-Andersen Welfare Typology - Universal vs targeted welfare models
  • Norris & Inglehart Secularization - Religion and politics dimensions
  • Multiculturalism Theory - Integration and pluralism literature

Ideology Matching

At the end of the test, you'll be matched to the closest political ideology based on Euclidean distance across all sixteen dimensions. This is approximate—political ideologies are complex and don't always fit neatly into categories.
